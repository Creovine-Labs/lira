# Lira Outreach Tracker

## Current Sprint

**Focus channel:** Instagram DM

**Why Instagram first:** you are verified, you already have personal credibility there, and many service businesses actively use Instagram for customer inquiries. This gives you a better chance of getting attention than cold email or LinkedIn right now.

**Do not create yet:** PDF, new landing page, payment plans, proposal templates.

**Use only if they ask:** website link, YouTube intro video, pricing details.

## Before Sending DMs

Make your personal Instagram profile credible enough for anyone who clicks through.

Do this:

- Update bio to: `Founder, Lira Intelligence | AI customer support for businesses`
- Add the Lira website link: `liraintelligence.com`
- Add one Lira story/post so visitors immediately see what you are working on.
- Tag the Lira Instagram account only if the account already looks credible.

Use `Founder`, not `CEO` and not `Building`. `Founder` gives authority without sounding too corporate or too unfinished.

## This Week's Goal

Reach out to **20 businesses** that visibly receive customer inquiries through Instagram.

Start with Kigali-based businesses first. Local trust makes replies easier, and physical follow-up is possible if a lead looks serious.

Search in this order:

1. `hotel kigali`
2. `apartments kigali`
3. `short stay kigali`
4. `laundry kigali`
5. `cleaning services kigali`
6. `clinic kigali`
7. `dental kigali`
8. `tour company rwanda`
9. `travel agency kigali`
10. `training center kigali`

Pick leads that show at least 3 of these signs:

- Customers ask questions in comments.
- Bio says `DM to book`, `WhatsApp`, `Call to book`, `Reservations`, or `Inquiries`.
- They post often enough to show the business is active.
- They sell something with repeated questions: price, availability, booking, delivery, location, requirements, appointment times.
- They have a WhatsApp/contact button.
- They look owner-operated or small-team, not a huge brand with a formal support department.

Skip for now:

- Pages with no recent posts in the last 60 days.
- Pages that look like personal lifestyle pages, not active businesses.
- Huge companies where the DM is likely handled by a social media intern.
- Businesses where customer inquiries are not obvious.

Follower count is not the main filter. A business with 1,000 active local followers can be better than a business with 100,000 passive followers.

## First DM Structure

Do not pitch everything. Start a conversation.

Use this structure for Kigali businesses:

> Hi [Name/team], I came across [Company] and noticed customers likely ask you about [specific thing].
>
> I work on Lira Intelligence, a Rwanda-registered AI customer support platform for businesses.
>
> I think there may be a useful fit for handling repeated questions like [specific example]. Would it be okay if I shared a quick example for [Company]?

Only say `I checked your website` if you actually checked their website. If you only looked at Instagram, say `I came across your page`.

## Example DM

> Hi [Company/team], I came across your page and noticed customers likely message you often about bookings, availability, prices, and service details.
>
> I work on Lira Intelligence, a Rwanda-registered AI customer support platform for businesses.
>
> I think there may be a useful fit for handling those repeated questions before your team steps in. Would it be okay if I shared a quick example for your business?
