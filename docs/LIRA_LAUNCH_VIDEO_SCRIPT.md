# Lira AI — Product Launch Video Script

**Target Duration:** 2 minutes 30 seconds  
**Format:** Handheld / casual, intercutting between two locations  
**Tone:** Authentic, founder-energy, natural — not corporate

---

## CAST

| Person      | Role        | Location                  |
| ----------- | ----------- | ------------------------- |
| **Abraham** | Co-founder  | Walking outside on a road |
| **Sarah**   | Co-founder  | Lying on couch at home    |
| **Lira**    | AI Teammate | Voice + screen recording  |

---

## PRODUCTION NOTE

> The first scene should look and feel like a completely normal meeting. The audience has no idea what the video is about. No titles, no branding yet. Just two people on a call. Let it breathe.

---

---

## SCENE 1 — THE MEETING

**Duration: ~0:00 – 0:38**

---

_Camera opens on Abraham walking on a road — outside, natural light, phone in hand or AirPods in. He's talking mid-conversation, fully natural._

_Cut to Sarah on her couch at home — relaxed, lying down, phone or laptop balanced._

_The two locations intercut naturally throughout this scene. Audience thinks: "This is just a meeting."_

---

**ABRAHAM** _(walking, casual energy)_  
"Okay, so the product is powerful — but we need a clear angle for the launch."

**SARAH** _(on couch, nodding)_  
"Yeah exactly. Because right now it sounds like just a meeting assistant…  
but it's actually way more than that."

**ABRAHAM**  
"Right. It can execute tasks, pull data, create stuff —  
it's basically like having an actual AI teammate in the room."

**SARAH**  
"Exactly. So how do we explain that simply?"

_Abraham slows down. Thinking face._

**ABRAHAM**  
"Hmm…"

**SARAH**  
"Maybe we position it as an AI meeting agent?"

**ABRAHAM**  
"Yeah but that doesn't capture the execution part."

**SARAH**  
"Or the cross-team use cases."

_Abraham stops walking completely._

**ABRAHAM**  
"Yeah… wait…"

_Pause. Both quiet. The silence is intentional._

**SARAH**  
"Okay… so how do we actually frame this?"

_More silence. Both staring._

**ABRAHAM** _(slowly)_  
"…I don't know."

**SARAH**  
"Yeah."

_Beat. Dead stop. That's the wall._

---

---

## SCENE 2 — BREAKING THE FOURTH WALL

**Duration: ~0:38 – 0:50**

---

_Quick cuts. Both now speaking directly to camera. Fast rhythm._

**SARAH** _(straight to camera)_  
"This happens in every meeting."

**ABRAHAM** _(straight to camera)_  
"Teams hit a thinking wall."

**SARAH**  
"That's exactly why we built Lira."

---

---

## SCENE 3 — LIRA JOINS

**Duration: ~0:50 – 1:00**

---

_Screen recording. Clean, smooth._

_We see a meeting interface (similar to what they were just on)._  
\*Cursor moves. Clicks **"Add Lira to Meeting."\***  
_Lira's tile appears — audio waveform activates._

---

---

## SCENE 4 — LIRA SPEAKS

**Duration: ~1:00 – 1:25**

---

_Still in the meeting interface. Lira's voice is calm, intelligent, natural._

**LIRA** _(AI voice — warm, not robotic)_  
"Hi Abraham. Hi Sarah.  
I think I can help with the positioning problem."

_Abraham and Sarah exchange a glance — slight surprise, then interested._

**LIRA**  
"Based on your discussion, here's the core idea:

Lira is a real-time AI teammate —  
that helps teams think, decide, and execute work  
instantly, inside meetings."

_As Lira speaks — demo overlay footage plays:_

- _Lira generating a positioning statement live_
- _Lira writing marketing copy_
- _Lira building a content brief_
- _Lira flagging action items and assigning tasks_

**SARAH** _(natural reaction, nodding slowly)_  
"Yeah. That's exactly it."

**ABRAHAM**  
"That's it."

---

---

## SCENE 5 — FOUNDERS EXPLAIN LIRA

**Duration: ~1:25 – 1:58**

---

_Intercutting between Abraham and Sarah — casual, founders talking._  
_Demo footage of Lira in action plays as a background / overlay._  
_This section should feel like a conversation, not a pitch._

---

**SARAH**  
"We built Lira because meeting intelligence shouldn't stop at transcription."

**ABRAHAM**  
"It should actually be in the room.  
Listening, thinking — and helping you move forward."

**SARAH**  
"Real-time insights. Task execution. Cross-team coordination.  
All inside the meeting."

_Demo: Lira live in meeting — surfacing context, creating tasks, answering questions._

**ABRAHAM** _(glancing at screen, pointing)_  
"And look — we actually built out the interview flow too."

**SARAH** _(leaning in, looking)_  
"Wait, that looks really clean. Who built this?"

**ABRAHAM** _(casually)_  
"I did."

**SARAH** _(smiles, beat)_  
"Right… I hired you as the front-end engineer."

_She looks at camera._

**SARAH**  
"Which means Lira can also interview Abraham."

---

---

## SCENE 6 — LIRA DOES INTERVIEWS

**Duration: ~1:58 – 2:20**

---

_Cut to: Video call interface._  
_Lira is on one side — structured, professional._  
_Abraham is on the other side — now "the candidate."_

---

**LIRA**  
"Hi Abraham.  
We'd like to interview you for the Front-End Engineer position.  
Are you ready to get started?"

**ABRAHAM** _(slight smirk)_  
"…Yeah. Let's go."

_Demo overlay:_

- _Real-time response evaluation_
- _Live candidate brief being generated_
- _Interview summary at the end_

---

---

## SCENE 7 — CLOSING

**Duration: ~2:20 – 2:30**

---

_Final shot: Abraham and Sarah, casual, side by side or intercutting — looking at camera._

**SARAH**  
"From meetings to hiring —"

**ABRAHAM**  
"Lira is your AI teammate."

_Lira logo appears. Clean. No clutter._

_Tagline fades in:_

---

> **"Lira. Think together. Move faster."**

# EXTENDED ANGLES

### Three additional story formats — short, standalone, natural

> These can be released individually as short-form content, or cut together into a longer version of the main video. Each one follows the same structure: **human moment → wall → Lira solves it.** Inspired by how Anthropic tells Claude's story — no jargon, just real problems made simple.

---

---

## ANGLE 1 — "I Joined Late"

**The problem: Missing context from a meeting already in progress**

---

_Visual: Someone — could be Sarah, could be a third person — scrambling to join a meeting that's already been going for 15 minutes. Slightly stressed, headphones in, quickly opening a laptop._

---

**SARAH** _(whispering, to the call)_  
"Hey — sorry I'm late, what did I miss?"

_Silence from the other person. They start to explain, fumble a bit._

**OTHER PERSON** _(uncertain)_  
"Uh… we were talking about the Q2 roadmap and… the API thing, and—"

_Sarah's face: politely listening, quietly lost._

_Cut to: Lira already in the meeting. Text on screen: "Lira — catch me up."_

**LIRA**  
"Sarah, here's what you missed:  
The team aligned on shipping the API in Q2.  
Two open questions — pricing tier and partner access.  
That's what you're deciding right now."

_Sarah nods once._

**SARAH** _(to the group, fully in it now)_  
"Okay. I'd push for a freemium tier. Here's why—"

_She's immediately in the conversation. No one waited. No recap needed._

_Beat. Title card:_

> **"Never miss a moment — even when you're late."**

---

---

## ANGLE 2 — "The Action Items Graveyard"

**The problem: Decisions get made in meetings. Nothing actually gets done.**

---

_Visual: Abraham sitting at his desk after a meeting ends. Tired, staring at some notes. Classic post-meeting energy._

_He types into a doc: "Follow ups from today…"_

_Then stops._

_He looks at the list. There are six things. He doesn't know who owns what._

---

**ABRAHAM** _(to himself, quietly)_  
"Did we say Marcus was doing the landing page or was that me?"

_Stares. Shrugs slightly. Closes laptop._

_Cut to: A different meeting — same kind of moment. Except this time, Lira is there._

_As the meeting wraps, Lira speaks — no prompt needed._

**LIRA**  
"Before you go — here's what came out of this meeting:  
Marcus owns the landing page, due Friday.  
Abraham is following up with the design team by tomorrow.  
Sarah will send the pricing doc to the client today."

_Three people on the call nod. Someone says "perfect."_

_Cut to: Slack. Lira has already sent each person their task. Individually. Automatically._

_Abraham's phone buzzes. He sees the message. Smiles._

_Beat. Title card:_

> **"Decisions made. Work actually done."**

---

---

## ANGLE 3 — "The New Hire"

**The problem: Onboarding a new team member takes weeks — because knowledge lives everywhere and nowhere.**

---

_Visual: A new person — clearly their first week. They're on a call, slightly overwhelmed, taking notes furiously. Someone is explaining the product._

---

**TEAM MEMBER** _(explaining, fast)_  
"So our API handles the meeting audio, which feeds into the WebSocket layer, and that's where Nova Sonic processes — yeah it's a bit complex, there's a doc somewhere—"

_New person nods. They're writing. They do not fully understand._

_After the call, they're alone. They open a folder. Dozens of files._

_They type into a search bar: "How does audio processing work in Lira?"_

_Nothing useful comes back._

_Cut to: Same new person, different day. This time, Lira is in the meeting._

_After the call, they just ask:_

**NEW PERSON**  
"Lira — how does the audio pipeline work?"

**LIRA**  
"The meeting audio streams in real-time through the WebSocket layer.  
Nova Sonic processes it for speech recognition and understanding.  
That context then feeds into task extraction and the org knowledge base.  
There's a detailed doc on this — want me to pull it up?"

_New person exhales._

**NEW PERSON** _(quietly)_  
"Yeah. That's exactly what I needed."

_Beat. Title card:_

> **"Every new hire. Up to speed instantly."**

## NOTES ON THE EXTENDED ANGLES

**Why these three:**

- **"I Joined Late"** → hits async, distributed teams, remote work — extremely relatable
- **"The Action Items Graveyard"** → the #1 thing people complain about after every meeting — universal pain
- **"The New Hire"** → org knowledge base, onboarding, institutional memory — shows Lira is more than a meeting bot

**Filming approach (keep it simple):**

- Each angle should feel like it was filmed naturally, not staged
- No more than 2–3 shots per angle
- The Lira voice + screen demo does the heavy lifting — the human moment just sets up the feeling
- Title cards at the end of each angle should be plain text, clean background, 2 seconds max

## POST-PRODUCTION NOTES

### Pacing Targets

| Scene                   | Target Duration |
| ----------------------- | --------------- |
| Scene 1 — The Meeting   | ~38 seconds     |
| Scene 2 — Transition    | ~12 seconds     |
| Scene 3 — Lira Joins    | ~10 seconds     |
| Scene 4 — Lira Speaks   | ~25 seconds     |
| Scene 5 — Founders Talk | ~33 seconds     |
| Scene 6 — Interviews    | ~22 seconds     |
| Scene 7 — Closing       | ~10 seconds     |
| **Total**               | **~2:30**       |

### Visual Style

- **Scene 1:** Handheld, natural light. Let it feel like a real call.
- **Scenes 2–5:** Slightly tighter framing. Still authentic.
- **Screen recordings:** Smooth, intentional cursor movement. No rush.
- **Demos:** Clean UI, full screen or split with founder reaction.

### Audio Notes

- Lira's voice should feel intelligent but natural — not robotic
- Scene 1 silence is intentional — do NOT cut it or add music there
- Light background music can enter at Scene 2 transition
- Music should build slightly under Scene 4 and resolve at the tagline

### Key Moments to Get Right on Camera

1. **Scene 1 — the pause.** When Abraham stops walking and they both go quiet. This is the emotional core. Don't rush it.
2. **Scene 4 — the reaction.** When Lira speaks, Abraham and Sarah's reaction should be natural surprise — not acted.
3. **Scene 5 — "I hired you."** This beat needs perfect comic timing. Sarah looks at camera after the line.
