## Leverage Model

Working theory:

```text
Early life is mostly effort: learning, repetition, and skill accumulation.
Later progress depends more on leverage: skill, network, and capital applied to the right opportunity.
```

Verification:

This theory is directionally true, but incomplete. It matches established thinking around career capital, specific knowledge, and leverage. Naval Ravikant's model separates leverage into labor, capital, code, and media, while career capital models emphasize accumulated skills, relationships, reputation, and experience. For Creovine, the practical version is:

```text
Outcome = Skill x Distribution x Trust x Capital x Consistency
```

Use the user's preferred three-bucket model for tracking:

- Skill: ability to build, sell, design, explain, and deliver.
- Network: access to trusted people who can open doors, validate, advise, buy, or fund.
- Capital: runway, cash discipline, ability to invest in product, travel, demos, staff, and time.
