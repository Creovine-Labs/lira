## The Most Important Truth About First Contact

Before the channel question (email? LinkedIn? DM?), there is a more important question:

**Are you a stranger or a familiar face when you reach out?**

The best salespeople at Gong, HubSpot, and Salesforce have a zero-stranger policy. They engineer their presence into their prospect's world *before* they ever send a message. By the time they reach out, the prospect has seen their name, read their post, or interacted with their content at least once.

That single shift — **stranger → familiar face** — is what separates a 2% reply rate from a 25% reply rate.

Everything in this document is about achieving that before your first message lands.

## Part 1: WHERE They Are — The Exact Channels & Communities

Your buyers are **Head of CX, VP Customer Success, COO, or Founder at a 20–500 person BFSI/fintech company.** Here is exactly where they spend their professional attention:

### Tier 1 — Primary Channels (High Signal, Go Here First)

**1. LinkedIn**
This is where BFSI decision-makers live professionally. 80% of B2B leads from social media come from LinkedIn (LinkedIn internal data). For a fintech VP of Customer Success — this is their professional identity. They post. They comment. They read.

**How to find them:**
- LinkedIn Search: Filter by Title: `"Head of Customer Experience" OR "VP Customer Success" OR "Director of Operations"` + Industry: `Financial Services` OR `Fintech` + Company size: `51–200` or `201–500`
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator (if you can access it): Build a saved search and get alerts when new people match your ICP
- Look at who follows/engages with Intercom, Zendesk, and Freshdesk's LinkedIn pages — those are people actively interested in support tooling

**2. Twitter / X**
Fintech founders, CX leaders, and operators are highly active on Twitter/X. Many of the most vocal buyers in this space share their frustrations about support tools publicly. This is where they vent, debate, and discover.

**How to find them:**
- Search: `"Intercom" + "frustrating"` or `"Zendesk" + "expensive"` or `"customer support" + "fintech"`
- Search: `"we use Intercom"` or `"anyone tried Freshdesk"`
- Lists: Follow curated fintech founder lists — many are public. Start with: Fintech Twitter, FinTech Founders UK, African Fintech community
- Save searches for trigger phrases: `"support tickets piling up"`, `"CSAT dropping"`, `"support team can't keep up"`

**3. Fintech Slack Communities**
These are private but accessible. Decision-makers share their real problems here — no filters, no PR spin.

| Community | How to Join | What You'll Find |
|---|---|---|
| **Fintech Founders** (fintechfounders.com) | Application required | Founders, CTOs, COOs at Series A–C fintech companies |
| **Revenue Collective / Go-To-Market Alliance** (gtmnation.com) | Paid membership | VPs of Sales, CX, Customer Success across SaaS and fintech |
| **CX Accelerator** (cxaccelerator.com) | Free, wait for approval | CX leaders — your exact buyer — discussing tools, vendors, problems |
| **Product-Led Growth (productled.org/community)** | Free | Operators and product leaders at growth-stage companies |
| **African Fintech Network** (various WhatsApp/Slack) | Research by geography | If targeting Nigeria/African BFSI — direct access to relevant founders |
| **FinTech.TV / FinTech Futures communities** | Varies | UK-focused fintech operators |

**Rule for Slack communities:** Do NOT join and immediately promote. Read for 2–3 weeks first. Answer questions helpfully. Add value. Your first 10 interactions must give — not take. Only then does outreach land.

**4. Niche Newsletters & LinkedIn Groups**
Your buyers subscribe to:
- *Fintech Confidential* — fintech industry commentary
- *The Fintech Times* — UK-focused fintech operators
- *Tearsheet* — banking and payments professionals
- *Product-Led Alliance* newsletter — CX and product operators
- *CX Today* — customer experience decision-makers

Many of these newsletters also have **companion LinkedIn groups** or communities. Join and be visible.

### Tier 2 — Secondary Channels (Supplement, Don't Lead)

**5. Reddit**
Less applicable for direct outreach but excellent for research:
- r/fintech — founders and operators sharing frustrations
- r/CustomerSuccess — CX leaders discussing tool choices
- r/SaaS — operators comparing software

Search these regularly. When someone says *"We're growing and Intercom can't keep up"* — that is Lira's ICP expressing pain in public. Find their profile, find their LinkedIn, reach out.

**6. Fintech Events & Conferences**
High ROI per relationship, low scale. Prioritize:
- **Money20/20 London** (October) — the largest fintech conference in Europe
- **FinovateEurope** (February/March)
- **Seamless Africa** — if targeting African fintech markets
- **Fintech Connect** (London)
- **Slush** (Helsinki) — European startup ecosystem

At events: do NOT collect business cards and batch-email people. Have 1 real conversation. Follow up within 24 hours referencing a specific thing they said. That single interaction is worth more than 100 cold emails.

**7. Job Boards as Signal (Underused, Highly Effective)**
When a company posts a job for *"Customer Support Manager"* or *"Head of Customer Success"* — that is a buying signal. They are scaling support. They feel the pain. They have budget earmarked.

**Platforms to monitor:**
- LinkedIn Jobs
- Greenhouse (many BFSI companies use this — Lira integrates with it)
- Lever
- Otta (UK tech startup roles)

**Search trigger:** Set up a LinkedIn Jobs alert for `"customer support" + fintech` in your target geography. Every posting is a warm prospect.

## Part 2: HOW to Warm the Relationship Before First Contact

This is the step 90% of salespeople skip. It is the step that doubles reply rates.

### The Pre-Outreach Warm-Up Sequence (5 days before you send the first message)

**Day -5: Find them and study them**
- Read their last 10 LinkedIn posts
- Read their company's Twitter/X activity
- Check if they're mentioned in any fintech press
- Look at the company's job postings (gives intel on current pain)
- Check if they've written any articles, blog posts, or been on any podcasts

**Day -4: Engage with something real**
- Leave a thoughtful, specific comment on one of their LinkedIn posts
- NOT: *"Great post!"*
- YES: *"This is the tension I see across every fintech CX team — you can't hire fast enough to keep up with volume, but the tools available either over-promise or under-deliver. Curious how you approached the build vs. buy decision?"*

One good comment plants your name in their awareness. They will see your profile.

**Day -3: Follow + connect**
- Follow them on LinkedIn
- Connect with a personalized note (see the exact note formats in Part 3)

**Day -2: Engage again if possible**
- If they've posted again — comment again
- If not — skip this day

**Day 0: Send the first real message**
- Now you are not a stranger. You are a name they've seen in their notifications at least twice.

## Part 3: THE FIRST MESSAGE — What to Actually Say

### The Core Rule: Make the First Message About THEM, Not You

The single biggest mistake in cold outreach is making the first message about your product. When you do this, you've handed the prospect a clear signal: *"This person wants something from me."*

The counterintuitive truth (proven by Gong's analysis of 300,000+ sales interactions): **the best first messages lead with an observation about the prospect's world — not a pitch.**

---

### The 4 Types of First Messages That Work

**Type 1: The Trigger Event Message**
Use a real event in their company as the opening. Something you found from research.

> *"Saw that [Company] recently launched [product/feature/expansion]. Growing fast in fintech usually means your support volume is about to compound. How are you thinking about keeping pace without just hiring a bigger team?"*

Trigger events to watch for:
- New product launch (press release, LinkedIn announcement)
- Funding round closed (Crunchbase, TechCrunch, Sifted)
- New market expansion announcement
- New C-suite hire
- Job posting for customer support roles
- Company mentioned by a competitor or industry publication

**Type 2: The Observed Pain Message**
When you've seen them express a frustration publicly (Twitter, LinkedIn, Reddit, Slack community):

> *"Saw your post about the Intercom pricing change. You're not alone — I've heard the same thing from [3-4 other fintech CX leaders]. One of them put it well: 'It's expensive to tell me what didn't resolve.' Happy to share what a few of them are doing differently if useful."*

This works because:
1. You proved you were paying attention — not sending a blast
2. You offered value (what others are doing) before asking anything
3. You didn't pitch your product at all

**Type 3: The Insight-First Message**
Lead with data or a specific insight they don't know — that is relevant to their role:

> *"Quick question for a Head of CX in fintech — do you know what your cost per support resolution is right now? Most teams don't. Gartner puts the industry average at $8 per human-handled ticket. The interesting thing is almost nobody knows their actual number, which means they don't know where the real cost is hiding."*

> *"Worth sharing if you're not already tracking it — or happy to pull together a quick estimate for [Company]'s volume if you want a real figure."*

This works because it provides immediate value (a fact they didn't have) and creates curiosity.

**Type 4: The Mutual Validation Message**
If you share a connection or a community:

> *"We're both in the CX Accelerator Slack. I've been following your takes on AI deflection rates vs real resolution — you're asking the right questions. Building something that specifically targets what you described as 'answers vs. actions' and thought you might find it worth a look."*

This works because shared context collapses the stranger barrier immediately.

### LinkedIn Connection Request Note (300 character limit)

You have one chance to give them a reason to accept. Do not waste it with:
- ❌ *"Hi [Name], I'd love to connect!"*
- ❌ *"We have mutual connections and I'd love to add you to my network."*
- ❌ Blank request (worst — they have zero reason to accept)

Use the 300 characters to prove you know who they are:

> *"[Name] — saw your post about scaling support without scaling headcount. That exact tension is what we're building for at Lira. Not pitching — genuinely curious about how BFSI teams are thinking about this. Would value the connection."*

> *"[Name] — Head of CX at a fintech growing as fast as [Company] is about to face a support tipping point. Working on exactly that problem. No pitch — would just value the connection."*

### The Cold Email Version — When LinkedIn Isn't Available

Email is still the highest-volume, most scalable channel. But it only works when it doesn't read like an email campaign.

**The rules (from 20M+ cold emails analysed by Woodpecker):**
- Personalized emails with 3+ custom snippets: **17% reply rate**
- Generic emails without personalization: **7% reply rate**
- Campaigns with 1–200 tightly targeted contacts: **40% reply rate possible**
- Campaigns with 1,000+ untargeted contacts: **2% reply rate**
- Sequences with 4–7 emails: **27% reply rate**
- Sequences with 1–3 emails: **9% reply rate**

**The lesson:** Small, targeted, deeply personalized campaigns dramatically outperform large blasts. Always.

---

**Email Template 1: The Job Posting Trigger**

> **Subject:** re: your Customer Support Manager job
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Noticed [Company] is hiring a Customer Support Manager. Growing team usually means ticket volume is outpacing what the current setup can handle.
>
> Before you add another salary to the problem — worth knowing that [similar fintech company] handled the same inflection point differently. They used AI to resolve 70% of volume autonomously and saved the headcount for complex issues.
>
> Would it be worth a 20-minute conversation before you finalize the hire?
>
> [Your name]

> **Subject:** your Intercom frustration
>
> Hi [Name],
>

>
> Not sure if timing is right for you — but would a 15-minute look at how we handle the payment failure / KYC delay scenarios be worth your time?
>

>
> Worth 20 minutes to see if the numbers change for [Company]?

> [Name]

## Part 4: The First Message on Slack / Community Channels

This is different from email and LinkedIn. **Direct pitching in communities is the fastest way to get banned and damage your reputation.**

The community-to-relationship process works like this:

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1–3): Pure contribution**
- Answer questions in your area of expertise
- Post genuinely useful observations (not about Lira)
- Example: Someone asks in CX Accelerator Slack: *"Anyone have data on AI deflection rates vs resolution rates?"* → You post: *"Gartner has this — deflection (bot gives an answer) averages around 60% claim rate but verified resolution only ~30%. The gap is the problem. Happy to share the full source."*
- Now 100+ CX leaders saw you provide real value with real data.

**Phase 2 (Weeks 3+): Warm DM**
After you've been visible and helpful for 2–3 weeks, a DM to someone whose question you answered is not cold:

> *"Hey [Name] — jumped in on your question about deflection rates last week. Been thinking about it since. We're building specifically for that gap — where deflection is counted as resolution but isn't. Would love your perspective on how BFSI teams are evaluating this. No pitch, genuinely curious."*

That message lands completely differently from a cold DM on day one.

## Part 5: THE FIRST CALL — Opening Line When They Pick Up

If they book a discovery call, the first 60 seconds set the entire tone. The best sales openers from the top SDRs at enterprise companies:

**What NOT to say:**
- ❌ *"So, let me tell you about Lira..."*
- ❌ *"I'm excited to show you what we've built..."*
- ❌ *"We work with companies like yours to help improve their customer support..."*

**What TO say:**

> *"Thanks for making time. I'll be honest with you — my goal today is not to pitch you. I want to understand how your support operation actually works and whether we'd even be relevant. If we're not, I'll tell you in the first 10 minutes. If we are, we can talk about what that looks like. Sound fair?"*

This single opener does three things:
1. Removes all sales pressure — they relax immediately
2. Signals confidence — you're not desperate for the sale
3. Frames you as an advisor, not a vendor

Then go directly into SPIN questions.

## Part 6: The Numbers — What To Realistically Expect

Based on real data (Woodpecker 20M+ emails, Gong call analysis, industry benchmarks):

| Channel | Realistic First-Touch Reply Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn connection request (no note) | 15–25% accept | Low value — just a connection |
| LinkedIn connection request (personalized note) | 35–50% accept | Worth doing every time |
| LinkedIn DM (post-warm-up, 1–2 prior engagements) | 10–25% reply | **Best quality replies** |
| Cold email (generic, blast) | 1–5% reply | Not worth your time |
| Cold email (personalized, tight ICP, <200 contacts) | 10–20% reply | **The target** |
| Cold email (advanced personalization, 3+ custom snippets) | 17–25% reply | The Woodpecker benchmark |
| Community DM (after contributing for 2–3 weeks) | 25–40% reply | **Best channel for quality** |
| Trigger-event email (funding, job post, news) | 20–35% reply | Most timely, highest relevance |
| Event follow-up (post in-person meeting) | 50–70% reply | **Highest conversion rate** |

**The clear winner:** Multi-channel warm approach. Email + LinkedIn + community presence, coordinated across a 3–4 week window before the "official" first message.

## Part 7: The Lira First-30-Day Prospecting Routine

This is the daily/weekly habit. Consistency is the variable that separates $0 ARR from $10K MRR.

**Daily (30 minutes):**
- [ ] Check LinkedIn notifications — reply to any comments on your posts
- [ ] Engage with 3 posts from target accounts (real, useful comments)
- [ ] Monitor Twitter search for trigger phrases: `"Intercom" + "expensive"`, `"support tickets"` + `fintech`, `"customer support" + "scaling"`
- [ ] Check job postings for `"customer support"` + fintech on LinkedIn (5-minute scan)

**Weekly (2–3 hours):**
- [ ] Research 5 new target accounts from the 100-company list — find the right contact, find 1 specific fact about their company, write a customized first message
- [ ] Send 10–15 personalized first-touch emails from that week's research
- [ ] Follow up with anyone at Day 3 and Day 7 of the sequence
- [ ] Post 2–3 pieces of LinkedIn content (1 insight post, 1 opinion post, 1 "build in public" post about Lira's development)
- [ ] Contribute 2–3 valuable answers in the fintech/CX Slack communities you've joined
- [ ] Review who opened your emails but didn't reply → those are warm — do a LinkedIn touch

**Monthly:**
- [ ] Review reply rates by template — kill what isn't working, double down on what is
- [ ] Export all conversations that went cold → re-enter with a new angle in 30 days
- [ ] Update your 100-company CSV with current status for every active prospect
